# Track B Phase 1 Checkpoint

branch: codex/paper1-trackB  
tag: paper1-trackB-phase1-v1

**Result summary:** Phase 1 (20 seeds, p=200, fixed support size, decoy intensities low/medium/high) identifies a narrow useful low-\(\lambda_2\) band \([0.05, 0.10]\) in the high-decoy graph-aligned setting. In this band, Proposed shows strong smoothness gains relative to SparseNoGraph with no clear support-F1 penalty and clearly outperforms A-ManPG across recovery, smoothness, and connectivity. The benefit is tuning-sensitive and does not support a broad recovery-improvement claim.

**Reviewer questions:**
1. Is the narrow high-decoy low-\(\lambda_2\) claim strong enough to matter?
2. Does the paper now convincingly separate smoothness gain from recovery improvement?
3. Should the next scientific move be Phase 2 experiments, stronger statistics/diagnostics, or submission packaging?
